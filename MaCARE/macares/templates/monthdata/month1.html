8 months to go....!

* Make an appointment with your OBGYN to verify that you're pregnant

* Start taking prenatal vitamins: Prenatal vitamins are important for your baby's development, especially in the early stages of pregnancy.They contain folic acid, which can help prevent birth defects.

* Avoid alcohol, tobacco, and drugs: These substances can harm your baby's development and increase the risk of birth defects and other health problems.

* Eat a healthy diet: Make sure to eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Avoid processed foods, sugary drinks, and foods high in saturated fats.

* Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water and avoid sugary drinks and caffeine.

* Exercise: Moderate exercise is safe during pregnancy and can help you feel better. Talk to your healthcare provider about what exercises are safe for you to do.

* Avoid certain foods: Some foods, such as raw or undercooked meat, fish, and eggs, and unpasteurized dairy products, can increase the risk of foodborne illness. Avoid these foods.

* . Manage stress: Pregnancy can be stressful, so find ways to manage stress, such as meditation, yoga, or talking to a counselor.
