6 months to go....!

* Your healthcare provider may recommend certain prenatal tests, such as the NIPT (non-invasive prenatal testing) or CVS (chorionic villus sampling).

*Eat a healthy diet: Make sure you're getting plenty of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Avoid processed foods, sugary drinks, and foods high in saturated fats.

* Continue taking prenatal vitamins: Prenatal vitamins are important throughout your pregnancy, so make sure you continue taking them as directed by your healthcare provider.

*Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water and avoid sugary drinks and caffeine.

*Exercise: Moderate exercise is safe during pregnancy and can help you feel better. Talk to your healthcare provider about what exercises are safe for you to do.

* Schedule prenatal appointments: Make sure you attend all of your prenatal appointments with your healthcare provider.

*Manage symptoms: You may experience symptoms such as morning sickness, fatigue, and mood swings during this stage of pregnancy. Talk to your healthcare provider if your symptoms are severe or affecting your daily life.

*Start planning for baby: It's never too early to start planning for your baby's arrival. Consider taking a childbirth education class, creating a baby registry, and starting to think about childcare options.

*Avoid certain activities: During pregnancy, it's important to avoid certain activities such as smoking, drinking alcohol, and taking certain medications.
