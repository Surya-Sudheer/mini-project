4 months to go....!

*Consider a fetal anatomy ultrasound: Your healthcare provider may recommend a fetal anatomy ultrasound during this stage of pregnancy. This can help identify any potential issues with your baby's development.

* Your healthcare provider may recommend a glucose screening test to check for gestational diabetes.

* Take care of physical discomforts, such as back pain and heartburn if you experience any.

* Eat a healthy diet: Make sure you're getting plenty of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Avoid processed foods, sugary drinks, and foods high in saturated fats.

* Continue taking prenatal vitamins: Prenatal vitamins are important throughout your pregnancy, so make sure you continue taking them as directed by your healthcare provider.

*Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water and avoid sugary drinks and caffeine.

* Exercise: Moderate exercise is safe during pregnancy and can help you feel better. Talk to your healthcare provider about what exercises are safe for you to do.

* Start preparing for baby's arrival: Now is a good time to start preparing for your baby's arrival. Consider creating a baby registry, planning the nursery, and thinking about childcare options.

* Schedule prenatal appointments: Make sure you attend all of your prenatal appointments with your healthcare provider. They will monitor your pregnancy and answer any questions you may have.
