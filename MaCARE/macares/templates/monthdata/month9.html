Time has arrived...!

* Pay attention to your baby's movements: You may notice a decrease in your baby's movements as you near the end of your pregnancy. If you have any concerns, contact a healthcare provider right away.

* Finalize your birth plan: Review and finalize your birth plan with your healthcare provider. Make sure that you have made arrangements for transportation to the hospital or birth center.

* Learn about labor and delivery: Review the signs of labor and delivery, and learn about pain management options. Consider taking a childbirth education class to help prepare you for labor and delivery.

*Pack your hospital bag: Pack your hospital bag with all of the essentials that you will need during your hospital stay, including clothes for you and your baby, toiletries, and any necessary paperwork.

* Rest as much as possible: As your body prepares for labor and delivery, it's important to get as much rest as possible. Try to take naps during the day, and get plenty of sleep at night.

* Monitor your weight gain: Continue to monitor your weight gain to ensure that you are gaining enough weight to support your baby's growth and development.

*Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water to help prevent dehydration, which can lead to complications.

*Prepare for postpartum care: Learn about postpartum care for both you and your baby, including breastfeeding, postpartum recovery, and newborn care.

* Get support: Lean on your partner, family, and friends for emotional and physical support during this exciting time.
